Dungeon Keyfence (Slightly less unfinished version)

  • Originality: It doesn't require a mouse to play. At its current state that's pretty much it.
  • Technical Merit: There's probably too many 2d arrays that are trying to keep track of everything. I also used a phaser function to create a grid of key sprites for the keyboard.
  • Prototype Postmortem: I learned how difficult it is to make phaser projects without any good code or phaser example references.
  • Prototype Assets:

    • Castle image: castle 4 on Pixel Art Maker

    • Background image: Background from Mocah.org (probably a scam site with stolen art)
    • Knight image: Knight of Finlandia by Master484
  • Prototype Closest Other Game: Other tower defense games, as well as Plants vs Zombies.
  • High Concept: A tower defense game based entirely on your keyboard.
  • Theme: Medieval, war.
  • Mandated Variety: The game is a turn based strategy/tower defense game. Input is entirely through the keyboard, with the number keys selecting units and letter keys placing them. Randomness is how enemies are spawned.
  • Prototype Goal: The idea with this prototype is to test turn based strategy gameplay as all my previous projects were more action themed (and some players experienced a glitch where those games ran too fast).
  • Player Experience Goals: I want players to feel tension as enemies get closer and closer to their base while they scramble for their resources.
  • Gameplay: The goal is to protect your castle from the oncoming army. Pressing 1-0 will select a unit and pressing any other key on your keyboard will place it. Press enter to advance a turn, spawning new enemies and advancing them. Game will eventually feature more units (including ones that move in the same way enemies do) and enemies with improved ai.
  • Strategies: When more units are added, the key strategy is using whatever you can to defend your base. It's all a balance between saving up your resources for more expensive units or spending them on cheaper units when you're in tight spots.
  • Story/Setting/Premise: Enemies are invading a castle and you've narrowed them down to a narrow, keyboard shaped field. Stop em or you're dead!
  • Target Audience: Fans of turn based strategy games and tower defense games.
  • Play Time: Should only take a couple of minutes to play, depending on how you can (theoretically) defend your castle.
